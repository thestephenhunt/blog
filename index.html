Basic Blog

I've never had a website.

I've had poortfolio sites. Just ephemeral pages that lasted as long as I was in the job market. But never a personal site.

In recent months, I've been reflecting on my career and envisioning my future. I've thought a lot about where I came from when I started software development. I always imagined I would end up doing design. I loved art. I took the absolute maximum amount of art classes in high school. I started college as a graphic design major. I drew, I painted, I doodled. I did all the things one might think someone who loves art does.But, college, as it turns out, requires effort. And my undiagnosed ADHD brain made it real tough to focus like I needed to.

So, I hit academic probation. And dropped out.

I was 20 years old, and I had no idea what to do. I moved back home, got a retail job, and spent the next 2 years drifting around hourly jobs. I didn't really work on art. Or side projects. Just coasted.

Then, one day a friend calls me up and says, "You think you could do software testing?"

"Uh, sure?"

He gets me an interview where he's working. An app development company. I had no idea what I was doing. But they had zero QA staff. So, I got to blaze a trail.

I picked up a little C# and made some friends with other developers. Some in the app space, some in the web space. Then, came a move to Texas, and again, as luck would have it, I got an interview with a friend of a friend, who owned a web development company. At the time, I only knew some HTML and CSS. From high school. But amazingly they still hired me.

So I started web development using Wordpress. For two and a half years, I learned everything I could on the job. I grew enough, that I was able to get into another company as a junior developer doing Ruby on Rails. Then, it was Angular. Then React and Node. Then Vue and GraphQL. And now, I'm leading a frontend team.

So, now we are here. I've been wanting to build my own site for a while, and simultaneously get into blogging. The plan is this:

  • Blog about mainly development, but maybe other things along the way.
  • Progressively build this site from scratch.

You may notice this site looks pretty plain. It is in fact a single HTML file. With each blog post will come some upgrades to the site. I intend to build a micro framework, geared towards personal site usage. As I develop it, I'll do my best to adhere to the minimal amount of code needed to accomplish the goal. I want to keep things as simple as possible, but as close to the "trinity" as possible.The entire site will be open source found here. I've never built a framework from the ground up, so this should be fun. Feel free to post issues or feedback on the repo.

If you made it this far, thank you for reading. I'm excited for this new journey.

Keep learning.

-Stephen
